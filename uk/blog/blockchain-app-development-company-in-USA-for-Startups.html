Models of outsourcing

Outsourcing companies and outsourcing firms use a variety of outsourcing models. These models will influence the way your project is developed and paid for.

These are the four most popular models. You'll see that some models can be fully compatible with your marketing budget and marketing needs, while others might not. Knowing the differences between each model can help avoid misunderstandings and unwelcome surprises when you sign a contract with a company that develops software.

1. Fixed-price

A fixed price model might be your best option if you have tight budgets and need to make changes as part of project development. First, you and your client agree to a fixed price for the product development before it begins. This is a compromise that gives you the advantage of the reference price. The developer has agreed to work at a fixed price. This limits the number of changes can make after you have started development.

A modified version, the Agile fixed-price model provides greater flexibility for development. This is because the developer uses an Agile development methodology which allows for changes in the development.

2. Captive Unit

The Captive Unit model allows the developer to provide the object and also hires and supports the R&D team who will be working on your project. They provide continuous administrative, financial, and personnel management for your team. This role is limited to managing the team and paying salaries. This model is useful when you need to build a team to manage a complex This model is popular with companies.

3. Professional team

This model is ideal if your project involves constant development or if you intend to create multiple projects. This model allows a development company to create a team that matches the requirements of your project. This team is dedicated exclusively to you and your idea. This model has the advantage that it allows you to share your expertise with the team and acts as an extension of your IT or developer groups.

4. Accelerated Build-Operate-Transfer

The Accelerated Build-Operate-Transfer model may seem strange, but the idea behind it is quite simple. You want to create an R&D department in Western Europe. However, your company is not located in Western Europe. This model allows a development company to identify an existing R&D infrastructure and manage a team for a specified period.
